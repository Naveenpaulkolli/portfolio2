Artifact 1 — AI & ML Timeline

Introduction: A concise timeline of major AI/ML milestones to ground later work in historical context.

Description: From 1956 (Dartmouth) and ELIZA, through backpropagation and Deep Blue, to AlexNet and modern large language models.

Objective: Show understanding of how key breakthroughs shaped today’s AI/ML practice.

Timeline Highlights

  • 1956 – “Artificial Intelligence” coined at the Dartmouth Conference (John McCarthy).
  • 1960s – ELIZA chatbot (Joseph Weizenbaum, MIT).
  • 1980s – Backpropagation widely used to train neural networks.
  • 1997 – IBM Deep Blue defeats Garry Kasparov (search + evaluation).
  • 2006 – Deep learning gains momentum.
  • 2012 – AlexNet wins ImageNet; CNNs go mainstream for vision.
  • 2019 – GPT-2 shows strong language generation.
  • 2020 – GPT-3 expands scale and capability for NLP.

Artifact 2 — Customer Churn Prediction (ML)

Introduction: Predicting churn so a business can retain at-risk customers.

Description: Built a baseline model, then addressed class imbalance with SMOTE to improve minority-class detection.

Objective: Practice end-to-end ML (prep → train → evaluate) on a common business problem.

Process

  • Cleaned data; encoded categorical features.
  • Train/test split; applied SMOTE to balance classes.
  • Trained Logistic Regression & Random Forest.
  • Evaluated with accuracy, precision, recall, F1.

Tools & Technologies

  • Python, scikit-learn
  • imbalanced-learn (SMOTE), pandas, matplotlib
  • Jupyter Notebook

Value Proposition

Unique value: Demonstrates awareness of real-world data issues (imbalance) and how to fix them.

Relevance: Churn modeling translates across many industries.
